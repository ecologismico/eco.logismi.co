IEEEXtreme 6.0 Programming Contest – Kickoff event

June 21, 2012 @ 14:00 - 15:00

  • « Software engineering for the environment: Setting up a research agenda for the next decade (Talk at IDSIA)
  • iEMSs 2012 »

IEEEXtreme is a global challenge in which teams of IEEE student members – supported by an IEEE Student Branch, advised and proctored by an IEEE member – compete in a 24-hour time span against each other to solve a set of programming problems.

This year, the IEEE Student Branch of Thrace will support teams from Democritus University of Thrace to participate in the competition. Ecologismico group will actively encourage students to participate, and will advise them to prepare for the competition.

During this kick-off event, the IEEEXtreme competition process will be presented, potential participants will be identified, while we will organize preparation activities for the summer break.

About us
Software Engineering for the Environment is a research group (aka "collaboratory") of the Internetworked Systems Lab of the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at Democritus University of Thrace. Our team members also affiliated with the Centre for Research and Technology Hellas, and Athena-Research Center for research activities.
